Understand Products

A product is a financial instrument offered by a bank or other financial institution to their customers. Examples of products are a credit or debit card, a business current account, or a 25 year fixed rate mortgage.

An arrangement or customer account in Backbase Platform is an instance of a particular product. The arrangement is typically identified with a number, for example an IBAN account number.

A bank may have an existing system to catalogue products and store arrangement details. Developers need to integrate the existing system with Backbase Platform. In case you use DBS, it includes a mock implementation that showcase a pattern that developers can use to ingest existing products and accounts. This pattern is based on pushing transactions through an integration service.

The widgets display the type of product (or product kind). In case you use DBS, it comes with a predefined list of product kinds, see Products.

This topic explains the following about Products:

  • Functionalities:

    • List products

    • Select product

    • Display product details

  • Overview: Widget collections and DBS support

List products

This functionality lists all financial products. For each product, some details are included, such as the account balance.

The details shown differ per widget, see the description of the specific widget for more information and screenshots.

From the product list, bank customers can navigate to the product details and transactions overview, and to the manage products functionality. The list shows the products grouped per product kind. In the web widget, the product kind headers are collapsible. When expanded, product details are shown, such as account balance, credit limit, and accrued interest. For the native mobile and hybrid widgets, tapping the product opens the product details.

Select product

The product selector allows the bank customer to select a product from a list or drop-down. Some product selectors support multi-select. This feature is used in combination with other functionality, such as list transactions, initiate a payment, or list account statements. For example, in the Retail Banking web experiences shipped with the widget collections, the Transactions page has two widgets: a product selector widget and a transaction list widget. When the bank customer selects a different product, the product selector widget publishes an event. The list transactions widget subscribes to this event and changes its view to display the transactions of the selected product.

The product selector displays information depending on the product type.

Display product details

Display the details of the selected product. The actual details depend on the type of product.

Developers may extend the data model to add custom details. The displayed details differs per journey/widget.
