Understand FX Trading

FX, or Forex trading is the trade of currencies on the foreign exchange market.

FX Trading is offered on the Backbase Platform, and allows customers to trade currencies available in the system.

This topic explains the following about FX Trading:

  • Functionalities

    • FX Trading

  • Widget collections and DBS support

FX Trading

The customer will gain an overview of the current exchange rates including the price, the price change in points and percentage, as well as the bid and ask prices available in the system. Additional detailed information is viewable for any currency pair available in the system. The customer can view further details of the final price at the end of the previous trading period, the initial price at the beginning of the current trading period, the year-to-date return, the 52-week price range, and the day range.

The customer can check to see whether a currency pair is possible to trade, that is whether the customer has at least one open account for each currency pair. The customer can place trade orders for currency pairs, and review trade order details. The rates update automatically, with configurable intervals, or when the customer refreshes the page. Whenever a rate updates, the selling and buying amounts specified by the customer are updated automatically. A prompt asks the customer to confirm the new amounts, which prevents the placement of undesired orders.

Widget collections and DBS support

The FX Trading widgets do not have a DBS support. Developers integrate the widgets with a third-party backend service.
