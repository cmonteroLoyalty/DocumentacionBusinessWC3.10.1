Web widgets

Understand Payments describes the functionalities of Payments. The following web widgets deliver these functionalities:

  • Initiate payment

    • Initiate omni payment: Dynamic payment widget that you can easily configure for various payment types. It comes with out-of-the-box configurations for US domestic wire transfer, ACH credit transfer, SEPA credit transfer (beta), CHAPS transfer (beta), and Faster Payments (beta), and international transfers.

    • Initiate payment wizard: A single universal interface that guides the business bank account user through the initiate payment process. Supports SEPA credit transfer, Faster Payments, CHAPS, and international transfers.

  • Initiate US payment: ACH debit payment

    • Initiate ACH debit: Direct debit widget that you can easily configure and extend to create new debits.

  • Manage payment orders

    • Manage payments: View all payment orders for the accounts you are entitled to access after login.

    • Manage ACH debit: View all one-time and recurring debits for the accounts you are entitled to access after login.

  • Manage payment templates

    • Manage payment templates: View generated payment templates.

  • Stop checks

    • Request stop checks: Submit a request to stop the clearance of an issued or lost check.

    • View stop check payment requests: View submitted stop check requests.

Initiate omni payment

The Initiate omni payment widget is a base widget that is fully customizable with a TypeScript configuration to support different payment types. It comes with out-of-the-box configurations for common payment types.

Supported payment types

The widget supports the following payment types out of the box:

  • Initiate US payment: US domestic wire transfer

    Payment type for outgoing US domestic wire transfers.

  • Initiate US payment: ACH credit transfer

    Payment type for outgoing ACH transfers.

  • Initiate EU payment: SEPA credit transfer

    Payment type for outgoing SEPA Credit transfers.

Note
 The below payment types (CHAPS, Faster Payments) in the Initiate omni payment widget are currently released as beta.

  • Initiate UK payment: CHAPS transfer

    Payment type for outgoing CHAPS transfers.

  • Initiate UK payment: Faster Payments

    Payment type for outgoing UK faster payments.

  • International transfer

    Payment type for outgoing international transfer.

    This configuration features:

    • Show allowed currencies only.

    • Show allowed countries only.

    • Show exchange rate, indicative, between debit account currency and payment amount currency.

    • Show delivery options:

      • Settlement time

      • Cut off times

      • Transfer fees

      • Urgent transfer

    • Show fee sharing options:

      • OUR

      • BEN

      • SHA

    • Route the payment via domestic or foreign correspondent and intermediary banks.

    • Show a summary of amounts and charges.

For the international transfer type, the user may enter a Purpose of payment. The Purpose of payment field will have different formats depending on the country of the payment beneficiary, these are:

  1. An optional free text field.

  2. A mandatory free text field.

  3. A mandatory field, selected from a drop-down list of codes.

    1. The user is sees an explanatory description of each of the available codes.

The format is determined by receiving the sanctioned countries endpoint.

For certain countries, a Purpose of payment is a mandatory requirement, and some will require specific responses from the pre-defined list in the drop-down. In others, the Purpose of payment is entirely optional and can be authored by the payee.

  1. An optional free text field.

  2. A mandatory free text field.

  3. A mandatory field, selected from a drop-down list of codes.

Features

When making a payment, the bank customer specifies the To and From accounts and the amount, adds an optional memo, and sets the payment schedule. All those fields are configurable. See widget reference for details.

By default, the widget displays currency symbols (for example, $) for To and From account balances. However, developers can configure the widget to display currency codes (for example, USD) instead.

The bank customer may click the X Clear button next to the Transfer to section of the payment form to remove entered beneficiary details without clearing the entered information in From account and Payment details.

Developers customize the widget with either out-of-the-box, or custom configurations. See: Adopt the Initiate omni payment widget.

Payment templates

The bank customer can save a successfully completed payment as a template from the payment confirmation view.

The bank customer must give the template a name up to a maximum of 50 characters in order to save it, the number of characters remaining is displayed. A notification informs the bank customer if the template saves successfully.

When a bank customer initiates an omni payment, they may use a template by clicking on the Select a template dropdown. This shows templates that the user has access to and that are associated with the currently initiated payment type. By default, the first 20 templates show in the dropdown. The bank customer may search for the template by its name. The user is only able to view templates they are allowed to create a payment from, this is determined by the entitlements of the user.

When a bank customer selects a template, data saved in the template will automatically fill the payment form. The prefilled information includes showing the indicative currency rates at the time when the template was applied for cross-currency payments. It also includes preselected payment and fee sharing options. The bank customer is able to overwrite any prefilled data.

Note
 Find out which payment details are stored in the template in the DBS documentation: Manage payment templates.

Error handling

In the Payment entry form screen, if a user inputs erroneous data that is not the correct form expected in a field, the omni payment widget displays a Payment data error. The Continue button is unavailable until the user corrects each error. If there are no errors, the user presses Continue to navigate to the next screen. The widget initiates backend validations using the Validate endpoint.

If there are validation or technical failures, both an error message and notification display. Errors are stacked at the top of the Payment entry form modal, and separate notifications display at the widget screen’s top right. The page scrolls, automatically navigating the user to the first error in the stack.

In the Review and Submit form screen, the user can Submit, Edit, and Discard the payment. If they click Submit, the omni payment widget initiates the Submit endpoint.

If validation and technical errors are thrown, the notifications will appear both at the top of the modal screen and the widget screen’s top right. The user will only be navigated to the Success form screen if there are no errors reported. The type of errors thrown can be, for example, if the Submit endpoint is not found or if there is a breach report.

The user is notified of an error if the selected beneficiary account is the same as the debit account. While this error exists, the user cannot continue with the payment flow.

Breach report

If the submit payment operation is unsuccessful due to certain limits breach, the user sees a breach report. In the report, the modal displays amounts for these breached limits, their current consumption and submitted payment details.

The user may select See details to further understand the cause of the breach.

Closed payments

If a business banking user only has the privilege 'CREATE' attributed to their account permissions for a payment type, the user can only make closed payments for that payment type, and are unable to enter beneficiary information into the Initiate omni payment widget.

A user who can only make closed payments are only able to select a beneficiary from their own accounts or their contacts.

Once a beneficiary is selected, the user is unable to enter information into empty fields or edit and remove fields filled with predefined payment information. All predefined details are greyed out to mark fields as unusable.

These details are:

  • Beneficiary name

  • Beneficiary account number

  • Bank branch code

  • Country

    If there is a predefined country set in the country field, it is unusable. Otherwise, the user is allowed to select a country.

  • Beneficiary bank name

  • Beneficiary bank address

  • BIC/SWIFT code

The user is able to click the X Clear button next to the Transfer to section of the payment form to remove all beneficiary information, bringing them back to the initial state.

The Save as a contact option is unavailable to the user as the predefined beneficiary information selected by the user will already be a saved contact or own account.

If in the case that a user enters beneficiary information manually before selecting a payment account. If the account only allows for closed payments, an error will occur. The beneficiary details are frozen, and a red warning around the beneficiary selector will indicate to the user that they must use a predefined contact or own account.

Similarly, payments initiated from a payment template via the payment template selector will throw a similar error if the selected payment account only allows for closed payments.

The checking process for closed payments permissions is configurable within the Initiate omni payment widget, with separate configurations for Payments and Payment templates. The default value for both of these configurations is ‘Check not performed’.

Endpoints

This widget uses the following endpoints out of the box:

  1. Upon loading the widget:

    • Retrieve arrangements:

      GET https://ips_host:ips_port/gateway/api/arrangement-manager/client-api/v2/productsummary/context/arrangements

    • Retrieve contacts:

      GET https://ips_host:ips_port/gateway/api/contact-manager/client-api/v2/contacts

    • Retrieve payment templates:

      GET https://ips_host:ips_port/gateway/api/payment-order-service/client-api/v2/payment-templates

  2. After entering the payment details, the widget validates the payment order:

    • Validate a payment order:

      POST https://ips_host:ips_port/gateway/api/payment-order-service/client-api/v2/payment-orders/validate

  3. The customer submits the payment order:

    • Create a payment order:

      POST https://ips_host:ips_port/gateway/api/payment-order-service/client-api/v2/payment-orders

  4. The customer saves the payment order as a template:

    • Create a payment template:

      POST https://ips_host:ips_port/gateway/api/payment-order-service/client-api/v2/payment-templates

    • Update a payment order

      PUT https://ips_host:ips_port/gateway/api/payment-order-service/client-api/v2/payment-orders/{paymentOrderId}

Initiate payment wizard

The Initiate payment wizard uses is a UI that guides the account user through creating a payment in any payment type the bank supports.

In contrast to the omni-payment widget solution where the account user can define separate entry points for each payment type, the Initiate payment wizard widget offers a single unified UI that guides the user to the correct payment types based on the initial inputs. The widget also provides possible delivery options for each possible payment types such as instruction priority, transfer fee, fee sharing options, the execution timeframe, and the cutoff time.

This helps the user to make an informed choice while maintaining the control over the payment method. You can re-use the omni payment configurations for the supported payment types or methods to power the payment wizard widget. Payment types supported by the Initiate payment wizard widget are SEPA CT, Faster Payments, CHAPS and International transfers.

When the account user opens the payment wizard, they will see the following fields:

  • From account: Required, select an account.

    • Select shows all accounts the user has access to and are allowed to debit, across all supported payment types.

  • Amount and currency: Required, choose currency and a enter an amount to pay.

    • Set to default currency set up by the bank.

    • Change currency to a different available currency.

      • Most popular currencies displayed at the top of the list.

      • Displays country three letter ISO code and name of currency.

      • Search for a currency by its ISO code and name.

  • Transfer to

    • Beneficiary’s name: Required, select a saved beneficiary or enter a new one.

      • Shows all accounts the user has access to and are allowed to credit, except the account currently selected, in the From account field.

      • Shows all beneficiary accounts listed in users contacts.

      • Auto-populates beneficiary name, account, bank country and bank branch code, if available.

  • Country: Required, appears when initiating an international transfer.

    • Shows by default the beneficiary bank’s home country.

    • Change the country to another available country.

    • Displays the name and national flag of the country, and the country’s ISO code.

    • Search for a country by its ISO code and name.

    • Changing the country shall also change the account credentials required for each country. For example, IBAN for Belgium, or BBAN and BSB code for Australia.

  • Delivery

    • Frequency: Required, how often the payment is made. Once for a singular payment.

    • Execution date: Required, The date the payment shall be made.

    • Standard and Urgent delivery selection: Required, displayed with cost and time taken to deliver payments.

The bank customer may click the X Clear button next to the Transfer to section of the payment form to remove entered beneficiary details without clearing the entered information in From account and Payment details.

For the international transfer type, the user may enter a Purpose of payment. The Purpose of payment field will have different formats depending on the country of the payment beneficiary, these are:

  1. An optional free text field.

  2. A mandatory free text field.

  3. A mandatory field, selected from a drop-down list of codes.

    1. The user is sees an explanatory description of each of the available codes.

The format is determined by receiving the sanctioned countries endpoint.

For certain countries, a Purpose of payment is a mandatory requirement, and some will require specific responses from the pre-defined list in the drop-down. In others, the Purpose of payment is entirely optional and can be authored by the payee.

  1. An optional free text field.

  2. A mandatory free text field.

  3. A mandatory field, selected from a drop-down list of codes.

Once the account user has entered all required details, the available payment type, or types if there are multiple, display. In the case that there are multiple payment types and the required fields for the payment types are different, such as ACH Credit or a domestic wire, the user is asked to select from different delivery options for each displayed payment type. Once the user selects a delivery option, the user is shown additional fields specific to that payment type. The user will see a cost overview for the transfer and the cost of selected delivery option. If available, the user is also shown a fee sharing option.

The account user has the option to add their own description of their payment for reference purposes.

The Continue button is unavailable until the user corrects each error. If there are no errors, the user presses Continue to navigate to the next screen. The user will only be navigated to the Success form screen if there are no errors reported. The type of errors thrown can be, for example, if the Submit endpoint is not found or if there is a breach report.

Payment templates

When a payment is submitted, the user has the ability to save the successfully submitted payment as a template for future payments. The user can select and apply a saved payment template, which allows the wizard to quickly prefill the details for a new payment based on the payment template’s saved information. When selecting a saved template to create a new payment, the user is only able to view templates they are allowed to create a payment from, this is determined by the entitlements of the user.

The prefilled information includes showing the indicative currency rates at the time when the template was applied for cross-currency payments. It also includes preselected payment and fee sharing options. If the template does not have a country, the widget prefills a country. The prefilled country is calculated as follows:

  • If the beneficiary account in the template is an IBAN, the prefilled country is extracted from the IBAN.

  • If the beneficiary account in the template is a BBAN, the prefilled country is the default country from the configured list of sanctioned countries.

The bank customer is able to overwrite any prefilled data.

If submit payment operation is unsuccessful due to limit breaches, the user sees a breach report listing these limit breaches. In the report, the modal displays amounts for these breached limits, their current consumption and submitted payment details.

The user may select See details to further understand the cause of the breach.

Features

  • Single universal user interface guides the user to initiate a payment.

  • Supports SEPA CT, Faster Payments, CHAPS, International transfer types.

  • Select from saved contacts or enter new payment details.

  • Save payment details as a new contact.

  • Set up a standing order.

  • Save a created payment as a payment template for future payments.

  • Search for payment template.

  • Select and apply saved payment template to complete a new payment.

  • Displays breach report when submitted payment fails due to limit breaches.

Initiate ACH debit

Initiate ACH debit widget enables the bank customers to create outgoing direct debits.

The bank customer sets the account to Transfer to in the following way:

  • Select an account from the bank customer’s own accounts.

The bank customer sets the account to Collect from in one of the following ways:

  • Select an account from a contact in the contact list.

  • Enter new contact and account details. In this case, the bank customer has the opportunity to add the contact to the contact list while creating a debit.

For all debits, bank customer can set the following:

  • Scheduled: plan the debit to be executed on a later date.

  • Recurring: plan the debit to be executed daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, or annually. The recurring debit either never ends, ends after a fixed number of occurrences, or ends on a particular date.

Initiate ACH debit uses the existing Manage business contacts widget functionality in the following ways:

  • Select a debtor from Manage business contacts.

  • Save a new debtor to Manage business contacts.

Features

This widget has the following features:

  • Create new ACH Debit in page view.

  • Review and submit ACH Debit.

  1. Enter ACH debit

    In this step, you initiate the ACH debit and define the following parameters:

    Mandatory

    Optional

    Specify the routing number of the account to be debited.

    Mandatory

    Bank name

    Specify the name of the debtor’s bank.

    Add bank address

Manage ACH debit

The Manage ACH debit widget displays an overview of one-time and recurring debits that the current user is entitled to access.

Pagination is available by default. Administrators can configure the widget in Experience Manager to load list items incrementally. To use this feature, the user clicks the Load More button.

The widget displays the following attributes for each debit:

  • Status: Displays the status of the debit as an icon. The possible statuses are:

    • ACC, accepted - specifies that debit is successfully sent to the core banking system. The core banking system is still processing it.

    • CAN, cancelled - specifies that debit is cancelled and will not be processed any further.

    • CAN, cancellation pending - specifies that the user requested to cancel a debit, but the debit was already sent to the core banking system. Awaiting response from the core banking system to confirm the cancellation.

    • ENT, entered - specifies that the debit is yet to be approved or rejected.

    • PRO, processed - specifies that the core banking system successfully processed the debit.

    • REA, ready - specifies that the debit is approved and ready to be sent to the bank.

    • REJ, rejected - specifies that the debit was manually rejected by an end-user, or rejected by the bank.

  • Collect From: Specifies the account from which the debit will be collected.

  • Transfer To: Specifies the user’s account to which funds will be deposited.

  • Ccy: Specifies the currency applicable to the corresponding debit.

  • Amount: Specifies the amount covered by the corresponding debit.

  • Exec. date: Specifies the date on which the corresponding debit is to be executed, if authorized. This field only applies to one-off debits.

  • Scheduling: The frequency with which the corresponding recurring debit is to be processed, including start and end dates. This field only applies to recurring debits.

Select a record in the table to display additional information about the debit:

  • Created by: Specifies the source account of the payment order and the date of the creation.

  • Created at: Specifies the date and time when the payment order was created.

  • Payment Reference: Specifies the customer reference for the corresponding payment order.

  • Description: Specifies the custom description of the payment assigned at the time of its creation.

To approve a debit in the list, the user clicks ✔ next to the item. To reject a debit, the user clicks ✗. A modal dialog appears where the user confirms their approval or rejection. To end the operation without approving or rejecting the debit, the user clicks Cancel. To approve or reject a debit in detailed view, the user clicks the Approve or the Reject button.

To cancel or delete a debit, click the …​ button and select the Cancel or Delete option from the drop-down menu. The Cancel and Delete options are only available if the debit has not had any approvals. The Delete operation can only be performed by the user who created the debit.

To copy a debit, click the …​ button and select the Copy option from the drop-down menu. This copies the details of the initial debit to a new debit. The user can now change any information on the new debit and proceed.

To print a debit, click on the particular debit to view its details and then click the icon.

This widget interfaces with the Payments DBS capability using the payment-order-client-api.

Features

  • View the list of one-time and recurring debits.

  • Load debits incrementally.

  • View details of an ACH debit.

  • Approve an ACH debit.

  • Reject an ACH debit. Click debit to see more details.

  • Cancel an ACH debit.

  • Copy an ACH debit.

  • Delete an ACH debit.

  • Print an ACH debit.

Manage payments

This widget displays an overview of one-off payment orders and standing orders that the current user is entitled to access.

The widget shows the payment orders in a list. The view shows All orders as a list by default, however the user can also select My orders to isolate payment orders that share the same user ID as the current user. The view will display a list of only the user’s payment orders. The lists are sorted from the most recently created payment order to the oldest, grouped by requested execution date. To sort a list by a different attribute, the user clicks the attribute in the column header. To reverse the sorting order, the user clicks the attribute again. The user may select the standing order tab to view automatic payments initiated by the accounts the user has access to.

Pagination is available by default. Administrators can configure the widget in Experience Manager to load list items incrementally. To use this feature, the user clicks the Load More button.

The widget displays the following attributes for each payment order:

  • Status: Displays the current status of the corresponding payment order as an icon. On mouse hover, additional information is shown. The possible statuses are:

    • ACC, accepted - specifies that the payment order is successfully sent to the core banking system. The core banking system is still processing it.

    • CAN, cancelled - specifies that the payment is cancelled and will not be processed any further.

    • CAN, cancellation pending - specifies that the user requested to cancel a payment, but the payment order was already sent to the core banking system. Awaiting response from the core banking system to confirm the cancellation.

    • ENT, entered - specifies that the payment order is yet to be approved or rejected.

    • PRO, processed - specifies that the core banking system successfully processed the payment order.

    • REA, ready - specifies that the payment order is ready to be sent to the bank.

    • REJ, rejected - specifies that the payment order is manually rejected by the end-user, or rejected by the bank.

  • From: Specifies the source account of the corresponding payment. order.

  • To: Specifies the counter-party account of the corresponding payment order.

  • Ccy: Specifies the currency applicable to the corresponding payment order.

  • Amount: Specifies the amount covered by the corresponding payment order.

  • Exec. date: Specifies the date on which the corresponding payment is to be executed, if authorized.

Standing and one-off payments

Customers are able to view standing orders, by opening the Standing tab.

Standing orders have additional attributes for each payment order:

  • Start date: The date of when the standing order initiates.

  • Frequency: The number of payments made in a length of time.

  • End date: The date of when the standing order will end. Only shown if there is an endDate property present.

  • End after (x) occurences: The standing order will end after a payments are made a set number of times, (x).

  • Next execution date: Shows the date of the next payment execution, only shown if data is present.

Filter payments

Customers are able to view specific one-off payment orders or standing orders by using the filter to isolate a subset of payments.

The filter uses the following criteria:

  • Status: One or multiple statuses, selected from the dropdown.

  • Amount: The amount, or a range specified in the min and max fields.

  • Execution date: A date, or a date range specified in the calendar.

From either the one-off or standing order tabs, the customer selects their desired filter options, the customer can close the filter panel without affecting their selection. If there are no payment orders found under the criteria selected in the filter, the widget shows an empty page. When the customer clears the filter, the listed payment orders refreshes, and the customer returns to the unfiltered first page of the selected tab. The customer can initiate a new search while also using the filter, the search acts as a further filter on the payment order list. The search or filter function does not override any listed payments the customer has already selected.

Approval of payments

If the user has the appropriate permissions, they are able to approve or reject a payment order or multiple payment orders at once. To use this feature, Approvals must be enabled in Digital Banking Services.

To approve a payment order in the list, the user clicks ✔ next to the item. To reject a payment order, the user clicks ✗.

The buttons will be visible or unavailable depending on the users permissions:

  • The ✔ button is visible and enabled if the user has permission to approve.

  • The ✔ button is visible but disabled if the user does not have permission to approve or if the user has already taken an action, but additional approvals are required from another user.

  • The ✗ button is visible and enabled if the user has permission to reject.

  • The ✗ button is visible but disabled if the user does not have permission to reject.

  • The ✔ and ✗ buttons are hidden to the user if the status of the payment is not "Entered" status.

When managing approvals for single payment orders or payment orders selected in bulk, a modal dialog appears for the user to confirm their approval or rejection. The modal dialog summarizes the selected payments. Payment orders are grouped by currency, and if they come from the same account, the summary shows the account details. If the payment orders are from different accounts, the summary shows Multiple accounts. The user optionally adds a comment of up to 140 characters to their action. The initiator of the payment and potential other approvers can view this comment in the Approval log section. To cancel, the user clicks Cancel. To approve or reject a payment order in the modal dialog, the user clicks the Approve or the Reject button. To print the details of a payment order, the user clicks the printer icon.

The following features help a user to manage multiple payment orders at the same time:

  • Select or de-select multiple payment orders on a page.

  • Select or de-select all payment orders on a page.

  • Select multiple payment orders across multiple pages.

  • Clear the selection with one click.

  • View the number of selected payments.

  • View the summarized information of selected payments in the approval flow.

  • Approve or reject all the selected payment orders together with one action.

  • Add a comment to a selected payment order.

  • Add a comment to multiple payment orders selected in bulk.

If the approval failed for at least one of the payment orders, the user sees a notification with a link to detailed information about the reason of the error, for example limit breach. If the approval failed for several items, the error message also indicates the number of the errors, along with a link to a list including detailed information about each error.

To withdraw a payment order, the user clicks the Cancel button. A modal dialog appears where the user confirms or cancels their action.

In the search field, the user can enter the following criteria:

  • Own account number.

  • Own account name.

  • Counterparty account number.

  • Counterparty account name.

Manage payment templates

The Manage payment templates widget allows the customer to create a payment template, view generated templates, examine the payment details of a selected template, and delete a template from the list.

The widget displays the following for each payment template in a tabular format:

  • Template name: Name created by the user.

  • From: Account name and number.

  • To: Account name and number.

  • Currency: Currency used.

  • Amount: Amount to pay.

By default, the list is sorted by Template name in ascending order. The business user can identify the payment type for each template, which displays with the Template name. Manage payment templates widget displays all templates the user is allowed to view, which is determined by the user’s entitlements.

If the business user has permission to create a payment template, they will be able to see the Create template button. Once clicked, the user is presented with a modal window, where they select an available payment type.

The user enters valid payment details in each of the modal’s fields. Once completed, the user clicks the Save button on the modal. If the details are valid, a success notification appears in the top right of the screen and the template is added to the list.

If there is a backend validation error, a failure notification displays.

The customer may easily isolate specific templates from the list by using the search function. If the employee’s search matches the information saved in the templates, the list displays only the matched templates, and the number of results found.

A template can be edited or deleted by clicking on the triple-dot button '…​' on a listed template entry and selecting one option.

When editing a template, the bank customer sees a modal view prefilled with the template’s saved data.

The bank customer may edit the data and click Save to confirm the changes or Cancel to return to the list screen, leaving the template unchanged.

The list of templates, and the details view of a particular template, allows user to create a payment directly from a template. Once clicked, user is redirected to either the Initiate payment wizard or the Initiate omni payment widget, prefilled with payment details saved in the selected template. The redirection is configurable and by default the user is directed towards Initiate payment wizard.

The prefilled information includes showing the indicative currency rates at the time when the template was applied for cross-currency payments. It also includes preselected payment and fee sharing options. The bank customer is able to overwrite any prefilled data.

The default redirection can be changed by updating the bb-payments-routing-container-ang container component. A switch case statement is provided, this is based on the payment type with three possible navigation values (viewPaymentWizard, viewWirePayment and viewAchPayment). The user is allowed to add all needed cases for each payment type.

@Input() set selectedPaymentTemplate(template: PaymentTemplate | undefined) {
  if (template?.details) {
    switch (template.details.paymentType) {
    //Cases for omni payment widget should be added here
      default:
        this.viewPaymentWizard.emit(template);
    }
  }
}

Request stop checks

In this widget, the bank customer submits requests to the bank to stop check payments.

If the bank customer issued a check, but wants to request the bank to not honor the payments, they submit a request to stop the clearance of an issued or lost check. This feature is helpful in the following scenarios:

  • The customer is short of funds, and wants to avoid the scenario of a bounced check.

  • The customer lost a signed check, and wants to prevent the bank from honoring it.

  • The customer lost an entire check book, and requests a bank to not honor checks that fall within a range of serial numbers.

To submit a stop check request, the bank customer fills in the check details form. If the request concerns a single check, the customer selects Single. If the request concerns multiple checks, the customer selects Multiple, and specifies the range of serial numbers.

To specify the reason of the request, the customer has the following options to choose from in a drop-down:

  • Insufficient funds

  • Incorrect amount or other details

  • Lost or stolen checks or check books

  • Checks mailed to wrong address

  • Dispute over purchases

  • Lost in mail

  • Other

Once the check details form is completed, the bank customer clicks Continue, and reviews the details of the request. To confirm the request, they click Submit.

Features

This widget has the following features:

  • Submit a stop check request for one or multiple checks.

  • Review the details of the request.
